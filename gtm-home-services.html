Ember Bots Go To Market Plan

Win local home service accounts with a done-for-you automation offer, not a generic platform pitch.

This plan repositions Ember Bots around local home services companies that use the most integration-accessible field-service platforms inside a 20-mile radius. The operating principle is simple: start where delivery is easiest, then sell measurable lead-response and follow-up outcomes that Ember can largely set up, monitor, and manage.

Strategic Reframe

The business should lead with revenue outcomes, while the CRM remains our internal operating system.

We should stop behaving like a software company waiting for product-market fit to magically appear. The near-term business is a local service engine: acquire qualified SMB accounts, deliver a tightly-scoped automation system with clear ROI, retain them on monthly management, and standardize fulfillment so Ember can own most of the delivery layer.

What we sell

Lead response, missed-call recovery, intake routing, quote follow-up, review generation, and appointment conversion for high-ticket local service businesses.

What humans own

Prospect meetings, trust-building, in-person sales, and nuanced customer conversations where relationship quality matters.

What Ember owns

Most list-building, content, SEO, ad copy, CRM workflows, quoting drafts, onboarding setup, monitoring, reporting, and support triage.

Initial ICP

Start with the most integration-friendly platform wedge, then prioritize one trade at a time inside it.

The first filter is platform accessibility. The second filter is trade economics. We want high-value jobs, urgent leads, real consequences when follow-up is slow, and enough margin to support a monthly managed service.

Jobber-first wedge

Best initial target. Likely the most accessible platform for early integrations and standardized fulfillment. Focus first on Jobber-based HVAC and plumbing companies.

Housecall Pro second

Strong next wedge when API access is available on the client's plan. Useful expansion path after the first template is working.

ServiceTitan later

Strategically important, but likely more gated and slower for early delivery. Do not make this the initial dependency for the first wins.

HVAC

Emergency demand, seasonal swings, call volume, booking urgency, and service agreements make HVAC highly automation-friendly.

Plumbing

Lead speed matters, customer anxiety is high, and missed calls often become lost revenue within minutes.

Electrical

Strong fit for estimate follow-up, scheduling, project qualification, and review generation after completed work.

Roofing

Longer sales cycle but large job values. Great fit for inspection requests, follow-up sequences, and estimate nurture.

Recommendation: Start with Jobber-based HVAC or plumbing companies first. That gives us the best balance of economic value, operational pain, and implementation accessibility.

Flagship Offer

Center the offer around what the client values most and what Ember can manage with low human overhead.

The best initial offer is not “custom AI automation.” It is a managed lead-conversion system for local service companies on platforms we can actually support well. Every feature should trace back to booked jobs, recovered leads, higher close rate, or lower admin drag.

Core managed system

  • Missed-call text back
  • Web lead instant response
  • Lead qualification and routing
  • Estimate request capture and follow-up
  • Appointment reminder and no-show reduction
  • Review request automation after completed jobs
  • Owner-facing weekly performance summary

Why this is the right wedge

  • Easy ROI story: more booked jobs and fewer lost leads
  • Operationally templatable across the ICP
  • Mostly text, workflow, routing, and reporting work Ember can own
  • Lower dependency on bespoke integrations in the first sale
  • Clear monthly management value after setup

GTM Engine

Use outbound first, while organic and paid channels are built on top of a proven local offer.

The sequence matters. The fastest path to learning is direct prospecting into a tightly scored local account list of integration-friendly companies. Organic and paid channels should amplify a message that is already proving itself in real conversations.

1. Local outbound

Build and score the first 150 to 300 companies inside the target radius. Lead with a concrete audit angle: missed-call recovery, slow form response, weak quote follow-up, or review leakage.

2. Local SEO

Create service-and-city landing pages plus proof-driven content around lead response, booking speed, and call recovery, but treat SEO as a medium-term asset rather than the primary first-90-day lead source.

3. Paid Google

Run small exact-match or tight phrase-match campaigns targeted to the 20-mile radius only after the outbound message and landing page are proving out. Keep one clear offer and aggressive negative-keyword control.

Delivery Model

Design the service around autonomous operations from day one.

Every new client should move through the same operating path so Ember can absorb the majority of the recurring load without turning each account into a custom consulting project. Platform compatibility is part of qualification, not an afterthought.

Standard onboarding flow

  • Account intake and baseline audit
  • Offer-specific template deployment
  • Messaging and call-routing setup
  • Lead-source and conversion tracking
  • First-week tuning and baseline report

Standard monthly management

  • Workflow monitoring and exception review
  • Copy and follow-up optimization
  • Lead and appointment reporting
  • Client support triage and recommendations
  • Escalate only when a human conversation is actually needed

90-Day Action Plan

Sequence the work so sales learning happens before scale spend.

Days 1 to 14

Nail the wedge and build the first account list

Choose the first platform-first ICP, define the flagship Growth offer, build a broader local research universe and top priority list, create one mini-audit format, and create one website page plus one landing page tailored to the offer.

Days 15 to 30

Start outbound and launch the smallest paid test

Begin personalized outreach and local direct prospecting. Only launch a small Google Ads campaign if the landing page, offer, and tracking are already clean enough to measure.

Days 31 to 60

Close first accounts and harden delivery templates

Use the first one to three clients to standardize onboarding, estimate true labor load, tighten reporting, validate pricing against real support demands, and verify platform-specific implementation assumptions.

Days 61 to 90

Scale the channel mix carefully

Expand local SEO pages later, refine Google Ads if economics justify it, grow the account list, and only add another trade or platform when the first wedge is closing and fulfilling cleanly.

Bottom line: If we keep the offer narrow, price correctly, and treat Ember Bots as a local managed automation service first, we can build a business that learns from real clients instead of waiting on product perfection.
