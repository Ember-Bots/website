Ember Bots Business Plan Presentation

Build a local home-services growth engine around a platform-first, outcome-led managed service.

This plan shifts Ember Bots away from leading with an internal platform and toward selling a high-value, repeatable managed automation system for local home service companies. The business goal is steady SMB acquisition. The operating goal is delivery that Ember can implement, monitor, and support with a high level of autonomy.

Primary Wedge Jobber-based HVAC and Plumbing
Target Geography 20-mile radius around home base
Business Model Setup fee + monthly managed service

Executive Summary

Ember Bots should sell measurable revenue protection first, not a generalized automation platform.

The right early business is a done-for-you lead recovery and response system for local home service companies. The CRM stays important, but as our internal operating system. Externally, we lead with booked jobs, faster follow-up, fewer lost calls, cleaner handoff, and managed execution.

The core strategic bet

Choose the easiest implementation wedge first. Early growth depends less on the total market and more on whether we can deliver fast, consistently, and profitably with low support drag.

What changes now

  • Focus first on integration-friendly field service platforms
  • Narrow the first trade focus to HVAC and plumbing
  • Lead with a managed service offer tied to missed leads and follow-up speed
  • Use outbound as the first GTM engine, with paid search only as a controlled second step

Initial ICP

Define the first ideal customer by implementation accessibility and economic value, not by trade alone.

We should prioritize businesses where integration is practical, lead value is high, response time matters, and the owners are not too small to pay or too large to be locked into a complex enterprise stack.

Platform filter

Highest Priority

Jobber first, Housecall Pro second, ServiceTitan later. The platform decision is part of GTM, not just implementation.

Business filter

Target established local operators, typically with 3 to 10 trucks or equivalent field capacity, enough lead flow to feel revenue leakage, and enough operational messiness to see clear value.

Geographic filter

Stay inside the 20-mile radius first. That supports local trust, in-person selling when needed, and tighter concentration for account research and outbound.

HVAC

Emergency demand, service agreements, seasonal spikes, high-value tickets, and strong speed-to-lead pressure.

Plumbing

High urgency, high anxiety, and fast lead decay. Missed calls often become lost revenue almost immediately.

Electrical

Strong future fit for estimate and scheduling workflows, but second-wave priority after the first wedge is proven.

Roofing

High ticket values and strong follow-up needs, but generally slower sales cycles and less immediate speed wedge than HVAC or plumbing.

Recommendation: start with Jobber-based HVAC and plumbing companies. That gives Ember Bots the best combination of practical integration, high-value lead flow, and repeatable delivery.

Offer Strategy

Sell a managed lead recovery and response system that maps directly to booked jobs and reduced leakage.

The buyer does not want generic AI. The buyer wants fewer lost leads, cleaner follow-up, faster response, and less owner attention burned on coordination. The offer should be framed in business outcomes and implemented in a narrow, repeatable scope.

Flagship outcome-led offer

  • Missed-call text back
  • Instant web lead response
  • Lead qualification and routing
  • Estimate follow-up sequence
  • Appointment reminder support
  • Review request automation
  • Owner-facing weekly summary

Best foot-in-the-door angle

  • Missed-call audit
  • Lead response audit
  • Quote follow-up leakage audit
  • Simple ROI framing: one recovered job can often pay for the service

Go-To-Market Engine

Use outbound as the first learning engine. Add paid search only when message, landing page, and tracking are already credible.

Early success depends on getting close to real buyers and learning fast. Local SEO is still valuable, but it should be treated as a medium-term brand and demand asset, not as the primary first-90-day lead source.

Outbound first

Research and score the first local account universe, prioritize the top cohort, use personalized audits and platform fit as the hook, and let the human team close.

Paid search second

Run a small, tightly constrained Google Ads test only once the offer and landing page are conversion-ready. Keep geography tight and messaging singular.

SEO later

Publish local service pages and proof-driven content, but do not count on SEO to supply meaningful lead volume in the first 90 days.

90-Day Execution Plan

Sequence the work so the first wins build the operating system, the case studies, and the evidence needed to scale.

The plan should not assume perfect margins or immediate scale. It should assume learning, tightening, and standardization.

Days 1 to 14

Define the wedge and build the first target universe

Lock the first platform-first ICP, build the initial research list, create the first audit format, and prepare one high-quality landing page and one outreach narrative.

Days 15 to 30

Start outbound and gather direct buyer feedback

Run personalized outreach into the highest-priority local accounts. Refine message based on real objections and what owners actually respond to.

Days 31 to 60

Close first accounts and harden the fulfillment template

Use the first one to three clients to estimate real labor load, validate pricing, tighten onboarding, and reduce support ambiguity.

Days 61 to 90

Scale carefully, not symbolically

Add paid search only if the economics justify it, expand the account universe, and do not add another trade or platform until the first wedge closes and fulfills cleanly.

Bottom line: Ember Bots can build a real SMB acquisition engine if we stop optimizing for platform ambition first and instead optimize for a narrow, credible, high-margin service Ember can execute repeatedly with minimal chaos.
