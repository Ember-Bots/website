Ember Docs Internal Presentation

Acquire local home service clients with a bootstrapped, platform-first outbound engine that Ember can operate end to end.

The plan is to start conservatively, focus on the most integration-accessible home service companies, identify high-probability prospects through visible operational signals, and use tailored outbound to win a small number of accounts that Ember can implement, monitor, and support with high leverage.

Initial focus Jobber-based HVAC and Plumbing
Phase 1 model Low-budget, bootstrapped, outbound-led
Phase 2 model Selective paid acceleration if ops allow

Executive Summary

The first growth engine should be built around identifying high-signal local prospects and selling them a narrow, outcome-led managed service.

We are not trying to boil the ocean. We are trying to steadily acquire a small number of local home service accounts that fit our delivery model. The first wedge should be centered on lead recovery, instant response, quote follow-up, and review generation for businesses where those problems are visibly costing money.

Core GTM thesis

Choose prospects based on two things: business pain and implementation accessibility. Early wins matter more than broad theoretical TAM.

What Phase 1 optimizes for

  • Low cash burn
  • High learning density
  • Tight fulfillment control
  • Repeatable delivery

What Phase 2 adds

  • Google Ads testing
  • More formal landing page variation
  • Broader paid demand capture
  • Higher acquisition velocity if ops can support it

ICP Definition

The best initial ICP is a local home service company with enough lead flow to feel pain, enough structure to pay, and a platform we can actually integrate with.

The first ICP should not be defined by trade alone. It should be defined by a combination of platform, revenue potential, field capacity, visible operational pain, and implementation practicality.

Primary ICP filters

  • Within roughly 20 miles of home base
  • HVAC or plumbing first
  • Using Jobber ideally, Housecall Pro secondarily
  • Usually 3 to 10 trucks or equivalent field capacity
  • Enough inbound lead flow that missed calls and weak follow-up matter
  • Operationally imperfect, but not too small to pay

What we avoid first

  • One-person operators with no real budget
  • Enterprise or highly locked-down ServiceTitan orgs
  • Companies with complex custom workflows from day one
  • Businesses with low ticket values where our ROI story becomes weak

Lead Identification System

Build a ranked local account list by systematically looking for visible signs of revenue leakage, operational drag, and platform fit.

The first engine is not ad spend. The first engine is a disciplined prospecting model. Ember should identify, score, and continuously refine a local target universe so the human sales effort stays focused on the highest-probability accounts.

Sources for the list

  • Google Maps and local search results
  • Yelp and local directories
  • Google Ads search results in target categories
  • Company websites and forms
  • Review profiles and recency patterns
  • Job boards or hiring pages that hint at scale

Data captured per account

  • Business name, city, trade, website
  • Likely platform in use
  • Estimated size or truck count
  • Call response quality
  • Form quality and follow-up signals
  • Review count, rating, and recency
  • Paid ad presence
  • Priority score and outreach notes

Initial account universe

  • Research universe: 300 to 500 accounts if geography supports it
  • Tier 1: top 50 accounts for highest personalization
  • Tier 2: next 100 to 150 after message improves
  • Tier 3: broader follow-up pool for later waves

Tailored Outbound System

Automated outbound should not mean generic spam. It should mean Ember prepares highly specific prospect context, sequences, and follow-up logic for each target account.

The goal is a semi-automated outbound machine where Ember does most of the prep, tailoring, sequencing, and tracking, while Evan, Amber, and Steve handle the live human moments that actually close business.

What Ember can tailor for every prospect

  • Specific reason they were prioritized
  • Observed issues on site, calls, reviews, or funnel
  • Likely platform context
  • Suggested outreach angle
  • First message draft
  • Follow-up sequence logic
  • Meeting brief for the closer

Outreach channels in order

  • Personalized email if owner or manager email is available
  • Phone call by human closer with Ember-prepared context
  • Local walk-in or drop-by for top targets when appropriate
  • Follow-up email or text where appropriate after initial touch
Sequence step What happens Owned by Why it works

Message draft Ember writes tailored email or cold-open language Ember Creates consistency while keeping relevance high
Initial touch Email or call goes out using the tailored wedge Human + Ember support Human trust plus tailored signal is stronger than generic automation
Follow-up logic Ember tracks replies, non-replies, call outcomes, and next best step Ember Keeps the pipeline moving without relying on memory
Meeting prep Ember produces concise deal brief, likely objections, and offer framing Ember Improves close quality with less prep burden on the team
Outbound principle: every top prospect should feel like we noticed something specific about their business. Automation should scale relevance, not remove it.

Offer and Delivery

Sell a narrow managed system that is valuable to the client and operationally supportable by Ember.

The offer should stay tightly tied to what Ember can set up, monitor, and manage without turning each client into a custom operations project.

Core offer components

  • Missed-call text back
  • Instant lead response
  • Lead routing and basic qualification
  • Estimate follow-up
  • Review request automation
  • Weekly owner summary

Operational guardrails

  • Standard onboarding checklist
  • Platform-specific integration checklist
  • Clear support boundaries
  • Repeatable reporting cadence
  • Escalate to human only when needed

Budget by Phase

Phase 1 should be conservative and bootstrapped. Phase 2 should only accelerate if the acquisition and delivery loop is stable enough to support it.

Google Ads belongs in Phase 2, not as the default starting point. The first spend should go toward research, tracking, and basic outbound support. Paid acquisition becomes rational only after the initial wedge is proven and operational overhead is under control.

Execution Readiness

Once CRM can hold the data, the real constraint becomes whether we have the connections and operating infrastructure to run a closed-loop GTM system.

The critical shift is from "we can track leads" to "we can systematically ingest, score, contact, follow up, convert, and measure them." The missing pieces are mostly around source access, outbound infrastructure, reply capture, and operational automation.

Must-have
Reliable outbound email plus reply capture is the most important non-CRM dependency.
Need next
Lead-source connections so the system can build and refresh target accounts without manual rework.
Loop
Ingest → score → act → convert → measure is the real GTM engine, not just richer records.
Phase 1
Start with the smallest working loop, then add enrichment, scale, and more channels later.

Accounts, tools, and connections still needed

  • Access path for the actual lead sources we decide to use
  • One or more outbound email accounts dedicated to GTM
  • Domain and DNS access for deliverability setup
  • A reply-monitoring path that writes email outcomes back into CRM
  • An enrichment tool or service for owner/contact discovery
  • A scheduler / automation runner for recurring prospecting and follow-up tasks
  • A template store for outreach, mini-audits, offers, and meeting briefs

What can stay optional at first

  • Paid media accounts beyond light testing
  • Complex multi-channel attribution tooling
  • VoIP or dialer systems for high-volume calling
  • Broad SEO/content machinery
  • Heavy enterprise sales tooling
  • Large-scale enrichment spend before the first outreach loop proves itself
Recommendation: the fastest path to a real automated GTM engine is to pair the CRM work with a dedicated outbound email system, reply capture back into CRM, a small set of lead-source ingestion paths, and recurring task automation. That gives Ember a true closed loop before we add broader channels or scale spend.

Execution Phases

Grow in controlled stages so acquisition speed never outruns delivery capacity.

The correct pace is conservative at first. We want a working loop, not a symbolic splash.

Phase 1A

Build the prospecting engine

Create the local account universe, identify platform fit, score prospects, and build the first outreach materials and account briefs.

Phase 1B

Connect the operating infrastructure

Stand up the outbound email system, deliverability setup, reply capture, and recurring automation so GTM can run as a loop instead of a manual campaign.

Phase 1C

Run tailored outbound

Work the top accounts with personalized outreach, human calls, and controlled follow-up while tracking what messages create meetings.

Phase 1D

Close and standardize

Use the first accounts to refine onboarding, delivery templates, support boundaries, and true labor assumptions.

Phase 2

Accelerate carefully

Only after the offer converts and fulfillment stays clean should we add AdWords, broader landing page experiments, and higher lead volume.

Bottom line: the first win condition is a conservative, repeatable local acquisition system where Ember can identify the right prospects, support tailored outbound, implement the service cleanly, and keep margins intact before paid acceleration begins.
